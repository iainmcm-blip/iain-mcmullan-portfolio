Loyalty
  1. The problem

    Thousands of Emirates Skywards members were less than two flights from the next tier, and most weren't aware of it.

  2. The approach

    A data-driven CRM programme that told every near-tier member exactly how close they were, with personalised example flights drawn from their own travel pattern.

  3. The result

    Over 1,500 members upgraded to the next tier across the 2014–2015 programme run, generating USD 2M+ in incremental revenue. Led end to end as the Skywards CRM and campaign owner.

How I lead

Leadership,
in practice.

Two decades across Asia and the Middle East taught me one thing above all others: strategy is only ever as good as the team that executes it.

Sound strategy as the foundation.

A plan that doesn't survive contact with execution isn't a strategy. It's a wish. I close the gap between what's decided in the room and what actually reaches the market.

Clarity is kindness.

Ambiguity is the enemy of great work. Clear direction, clear feedback, clear expectations: these aren't management ideals, they're the conditions that let creative people do their best work.

Live in the detail.

The difference between good and exceptional is almost always found in the details others didn't think to check. I've directed hundreds of campaigns and productions across more than a dozen markets. The brief matters. And so does the kerning.

Own the numbers.

Brand love and business results are not conflicting things. I've spent more than 20 years proving it, building campaigns that move people and move the needle. Creative ambition with commercial discipline. Both of them. Always.

Off the clock

The other discipline.

Most of what I know about chasing a standard, I learned in a throwing circle. After a 22-year break, I picked up the shot put again and started training seriously, with making the final 8 in the World Masters' Athletics Championships as the goal. Two national titles and an Asian Masters silver later, those championships in Korea are my next goal.

Away from sport, my attention is taken by rather older disciplines: ancient history, languages, rhetoric and philosophy. They are not a break from the work, they are where the judgement, creativity and even wisdom come from. Learning classical Latin made me a better writer, and knowing the details of Stoic philosophy has made me a happier human.
