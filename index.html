04 · How I lead

Leadership,
in practice.

Two decades across Asia and the Middle East taught me one thing above all others: strategy is only ever as good as the team that executes it.

Sound strategy as the foundation.

A plan that doesn't survive contact with execution isn't a strategy. It's a wish. I close the gap between what's decided in the room and what actually reaches the market.

Hire for attitude.

Skills can be learned but personality and perspectives are innate. The right people are those who bring the right attitude, with a hunger for development and accomplishment. I help to nurture both.

Clarity is kindness.

Ambiguity is the enemy of great work. Clear direction, clear feedback, clear expectations: these aren't management ideals, they're the conditions that let creative people do their best work.

Live in the detail.

The difference between good and exceptional is almost always found in the details others didn't think to check. I've directed hundreds of campaigns and productions across more than a dozen markets. The brief matters. And so does the kerning.

Own the numbers.

Brand love and business results are not conflicting things. I've spent more than 20 years proving it, building campaigns that move people and move the needle. Creative ambition with commercial discipline. Both of them. Always.

Move fast. Build to last.

Speed without rigour produces noise and mess. Rigour without urgency produces nothing at all. I've built workflows, teams and campaigns that move quickly, and hold up over time.

05 · Perspectives

What I'm thinking about.

Short takes on brand, loyalty and the craft of marketing leadership — drawn from twenty years of building them.

Loyalty

Loyalty isn't a points programme.

The brands that win treat loyalty as a relationship, not a transaction. Scaling Skywards past 20 million members, and launching My Family, taught me the real lever is emotional, not mathematical.

AI & Craft

AI won't replace marketers.

It will replace the marketing that was already replaceable. The leaders who thrive pair machine speed with human taste, using AI to reach the good idea faster, not to skip having one.

Brand vs. Performance

It's a false choice.

Across aviation, finance and hospitality I've watched teams burn budget defending brand against performance. The job is to build brands that also convert, and to measure both honestly.
