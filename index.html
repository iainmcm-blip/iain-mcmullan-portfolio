How I Lead

Leadership, in practice.

Two decades leading brand, marketing and operations across cultures taught me one thing above all: strategy is only ever as good as the team that ships it. A few principles I hold to.

rocket_launch

Strategy is a verb.

It only counts when it ships. I judge work by what reaches the customer, not what reaches the deck.

groups

Hire for judgement.

I build teams around people who think, then give them the room — and the standards — to get great at the craft.

visibility

Clarity is kindness.

Clear briefs, clear calls, clear feedback. Ambiguity is the most expensive thing in any organisation.

build

Win in the detail.

Big ideas are made real by meticulous execution. I sweat the production as hard as the concept.

insights

Close to the work and the numbers.

A leader who can't read the data or the room shouldn't be making the call.

bolt

Move fast, build to last.

Speed and durability aren't opposites. Ship quickly — but build brands and systems that outlive the campaign.

Perspectives

What I'm thinking about.

Short takes on brand, loyalty and the craft of marketing leadership — drawn from twenty years of building them.

Loyalty

Loyalty isn't a points programme.

The brands that win treat loyalty as a relationship, not a transaction. Scaling Skywards past 20 million members — and launching My Family — taught me the real lever is emotional, not mathematical.

AI & Craft

AI won't replace marketers.

It will replace the marketing that was already replaceable. The leaders who thrive pair machine speed with human taste — using AI to reach the good idea faster, not to skip having one.

Brand vs. Performance

It's a false choice.

Across aviation, finance and hospitality I've watched teams burn budget defending brand against performance. The job is to build brands that also convert — and to measure both honestly.
