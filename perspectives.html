Loyalty

Loyalty isn't a points programme.

The brands that win treat loyalty as a relationship, not a transaction. Scaling Skywards past 20 million members, and launching My Family, taught me the real lever is emotional, not mathematical.

Most loyalty programmes are discount schemes wearing a nicer logo. They buy a transaction, then wonder why the relationship never deepens.

The brands that win start from the other end. They ask what would make a member feel recognised rather than simply rewarded, and build the mechanics in service of that feeling. Points are the plumbing. Belonging is the product.

AI & Craft

AI won't replace marketers.

It will replace the marketing that was already replaceable. The leaders who thrive pair machine speed with human taste, using AI to reach the good idea faster, not to skip having one.

Every wave of automation removes the work that was already commoditised. AI is doing it faster and across more of the funnel, which is why the nervous people are usually the ones whose output was replaceable to begin with.

The leaders who thrive treat AI as a way to reach the good idea faster, not as a substitute for having one. Machine speed, human taste. The taste is still the job.

Brand vs. Performance

It's a false choice.

Across aviation, finance and hospitality I've watched teams burn budget defending brand against performance. The job is to build brands that also convert, and to measure both honestly.

The brand-versus-performance debate is mostly a budgeting argument dressed up as a philosophy. Each side defends its line item by insisting the other doesn't work.

The honest answer is that brands which convert are built deliberately, measured properly, and judged on both the short and the long number. The job is not to pick a side. It is to refuse the false choice.
